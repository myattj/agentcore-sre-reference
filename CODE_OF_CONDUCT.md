# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We want participation in the Agent community to be safe, respectful, and useful. Contributors, maintainers, and users are expected to treat one another with empathy and professional care, regardless of experience, identity, background, or point of view.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that helps:

- Give specific, constructive feedback about ideas and code.
- Assume good intent while remaining clear about impact.
- Make space for newcomers and explain project-specific context.
- Respect privacy, confidentiality, and security boundaries.
- Credit the work and ideas of others.
- Accept correction and repair harm when possible.
- Keep disagreements focused on the work.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following are not acceptable:

- Harassment, threats, stalking, or discriminatory language.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention.
- Personal attacks, insults, trolling, or sustained disruption.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Deliberately exposing credentials, vulnerabilities, or customer data.
- Retaliation against someone who raises a concern in good faith.
- Conduct that would reasonably make another person feel unsafe or unwelcome.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other spaces where someone is representing the project.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to a repository maintainer through an available private GitHub contact channel. If no private channel is available, open a minimal issue requesting private contact without identifying the affected person or publishing sensitive details.

Maintainers may remove content, lock conversations, reject contributions, restrict participation, or ban an account when behavior is harmful. They should consider context, impact, recurrence, and the safety of the people involved.

Agent is archived, so reports may not receive a prompt response. GitHub's own reporting and blocking tools remain available for urgent platform-level abuse.

## Good-faith reports

Reports made in good faith will not be treated as harassment merely because they are uncomfortable or critical. Knowingly false reports and retaliation are themselves violations of this code.
